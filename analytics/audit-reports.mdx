<h2 id="AuditingICC">Auditing ICC</h2>

<p>The <span>ICC Audit</span> report offers a chronological record of actions and changes related to SIMs across various portfolios. It records user actions, portfolio modifications, SIM activations, provisioning requests, and IP address assignments.</p>

<Tip>It enables thorough tracking and auditing of ICC lifecycle events and management activities. Allows users to monitor modifications made to ICC configurations, helping to identify who made changes, when they occurred, and what specific alterations were implemented.</Tip>

<p><img src="/images/resources/images/infinity/iccauditreport.png" alt="Iccauditreport" /> </p>

<p>It displays the <a href="#ICC6">ICC Audit attributes</a>.</p>

<h3><span>ICC Audit</span> attributes</h3>

<table> <col /> <col /> <thead> <tr> <th>Field</th> <th>Description</th> </tr> </thead> <tbody> <tr> <td><span>User ID</span> </td> <td> The unique user identifier. The user represents the human user or workload that can use the current portfolio. Users are assigned a type, which grants permissions and restricts access to system functions. </td> </tr> <tr> <td><span>Portfolio ID</span> </td> <td> The unique portfolio identifier. The portfolio is the overall customer account that contains user, package, network and SIM information, and includes details such as linked addresses, contact phone numbers, tax information and billing currency information. </td> </tr> <tr> <td><span>Date</span> </td> <td> The date and time of the change, formatted as dd/mm/yyyy hh:mm:ss. </td> </tr> <tr> <td><span>Reference</span> </td> <td> The <a href="/glossary/iccid">ICCID</a> of the SIM on which the user made the change. </td> </tr> <tr> <td><span>Information</span> </td> <td>The specific data or details regarding the <a href="/glossary/iccid">ICCID</a>. </td> </tr> <tr> <td><span>Description</span> </td> <td> Report template description. Notes about the report template, for internal use only. The report template description can contain details or provide other customer-related information. For example: <ul> <li>Anynet SIM queued for provisioning</li> <li>IP address assigned</li> <li>SIM Usage alert triggered</li> </ul> </td> </tr> </tbody> </table>

<p>This is the first page of the bill that displays a summary of:</p>

<p>If your estate manages below 1000 SIMs, a list of individual SIM cost is summarised in the bill ( PDF).</p>

<p> If your estate manages over 1000 SIMs, these records are not provided in the bill (PDF). In such a scenario, use <span>Detailed Invoice</span> (as shown below) to view the individual SIM records in detail.</p>

<p><img src="/images/resources/images/infinity/financereport2.png" alt="Financereport2" /> </p>

<p>If pooled usage is applied to the current invoice, the <span>Data Charges</span> are displayed in the billing report. If the usage is applied in excess of pooled allowance, data, SMS or voice charges are detailed in the bill. For more information, see<a href="/billing/billing-pooling#Understa" target="_blank">Understanding pooling</a>.</p>

<Note>Further invoice details are listed in the.CSV file, for example, the package that incurred the charge. </Note>

<p><img src="/images/resources/images/infinity/financereport3.png" alt="Financereport3" /> </p>
