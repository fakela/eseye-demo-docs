<h2>Establishing a cellular connection on the LTE Cat.1-xx click</h2>

<p>On the LTE Cat.1-xx click, bend the <span>RTS</span> pin outwards, or clip it off completely.</p>

<p>Ensure you bend the RTS pin, and not the RST pin.</p>

<p><img src="/images/resources/images/bendpin1.png" alt="Bendpin1" /> </p>

<p>Ensure you have connected the terminal antenna. The antenna connects to the MAIN Antenna connection point on the <span>LTE Cat.1-xx click.</span></p>

<p><img src="/images/resources/images/clickantenna.png" alt="Clickantenna" /> </p>

<p>To power on and establish your cellular connection:</p>

<ol> <li> Fully insert the micro SIM card into the LTE Cat.1-xx click.  <span><strong>Tip: </strong>The socket is a push-to-insert, push-to-release type socket. When the SIM is properly inserted, it clicks into place and sits flush with the edge of the SIM slot. </span> <br /><img src="/images/resources/images/clickinsertsim.png" alt="Clickinsertsim" />  </li> <li> Combine the LTE Cat.1-xx click and the Click USB adapter. </li> <li>  Attach a standard USB A to USB mini B cable from your computer to the Click USB adapter.  <br />The LTE Cat.1-xx click powers on. The PWR (Power) LED light illuminates on the USB adapter, and three LED lights illuminate on the LTE Cat.1-xx click. <br /><img src="/images/resources/images/clickpoweron.png" alt="Clickpoweron" />  <br />The LTE Cat.1-xx click connects to a cellular network using the Cinterion® ELS61 Wireless Module, and is now ready to receive security and identity information from AWS. </li> </ol>
