<p>SIM management involves overseeing a SIM's entire lifecycle, from activation to termination, while ensuring it is appropriately categorised and functioning as expected. This process typically involves:</p>

<ul> <li> <span>Ordering</span>: The process begins with placing an order for new SIMs, which will link the SIMs to the relevant customer account (<a href="/glossary/portfolio">portfolio</a>). This involves specifying the quantity and type of SIMs required, and assigning them an agreed cost structure (<a href="/glossary/package">package)</a>, ensuring the SIMs are ready for deployment and activation.  <br />For more information, see Managing Orders in Integra. </li> <li> <span>Deployment</span>: This stage involves assigning each SIM to a specific device, and distributing these devices for use in specific locations.  <br />For more information, see <a href="/portfolios/manage-addresses">About managing portfolio addresses</a>. </li> <li> <span>Activation</span>: When a new SIM is added to a customer account, it is configured for use on its available regional networks. Customers can choose to activate SIMs at any stage during deployment, either while they're inserted into devices in the factory, when the device is used for the first time, or after they are installed in their various global locations. An active SIM is a connected and usable SIM, so service charges are applied. <br />For more information, see <a href="/billing/billing-sim-lifecycle">Understanding the SIM lifecycle</a>. </li> <li> <span>Status Management</span>: As SIMs progress through different stages of their lifecycle, you can change their status according to their use case. For example, you might change a SIM's status to <span>Active</span>, <span>Suspended</span>, or <span>Available</span> (Terminated), depending on your business needs or contract terms.  <br />For more information, see[CDATA[ ]]<a href="/managing-my-estate/sim-change-state">About changing SIM states</a>. </li> <li> <span>Classification</span>: To streamline management, you can classify SIMs into collections, and tag them in groups. This enables you to organise SIMs based on criteria such as region, customer, usage type, or any other relevant factor. Classifying SIMs can simplify bulk operations and make it easier to monitor and manage large numbers of SIMs. <br />For more information, see <a href="/managing-my-estate/grouping-sims">Grouping SIMs</a>. </li> <li> <span>Adjusting packages</span>: If a SIM’s usage patterns change, you can modify the associated package to better suit the current needs. This might involve updating data allowances, changing the package billing structure, or renegotiating contract terms based on customer needs. <br />For more information, see <a href="/packages/managing-packages-overview">Managing packages</a>. </li> <li><span>Termination and reassignment</span>: When you no longer need a SIM, you can terminate it, or reassign it. This includes updating its status and ensuring that any associated costs or data are accurately accounted for.</li> <li> <span>Handling alerts and notifications</span>: Managing a SIM also involves setting up alert profiles to track specific thresholds like data usage or call volume. When a threshold is breached, you can receive alerts and email notifications, allowing you to take action promptly, such as suspending the SIM. <br />For more information, see <a href="/managing-my-estate/monitoring-sims">Monitoring SIMs</a>. </li> </ul>

<p>Through these processes, your system provides a detailed and user-friendly way to maintain control over each SIM's lifecycle, ensuring efficient usage, cost management, and responsiveness to customer needs.</p>
