<h2 id="AboutServiceContracts">About Service Contracts</h2>

<p>Service Contracts describe the agreed commercial contract between Eseye and the customer, including:</p>

<ul> <li>Where the SIMs will be deployed</li> <li> Services available – SMS and data <span><strong>Note: </strong>Voice is no longer offered, as it is not required for M2M communication. Voice is included in the billing topics for legacy reasons.</span> </li> <li>SIM purchase price</li> <li>SIM activation charge</li> <li> Billing and rating</li> <li>Bundles</li> <li> Billing currency – Eseye does not support mixed currency billing within a portfolio account <br />If you need multiple currency billing, you must set up a new portfolio account and a new Service Contract per currency. After a portfolio account is set up, you cannot change its currency. </li> <li> Contract length – describes the minimum contract term <br />When the contract length lapses, SIMs are not automatically terminated, and service will continue on a rolling monthly contract. An active SIM will continue to accrue charges. </li> <li>Customer notes</li> </ul>

<p>For information about setting up one or more Service Contracts on your Eseye SIM estate, speak to your Account Manager. Eseye will set up a package for you.</p>

<h2 id="Aboutpackages">About packages</h2>

<p>A <span>package</span> contains the information required to supply, provision and operate SIMs for a customer. Each package is a specific bundle of products and services, tailored to meet particular customer needs. It defines commercial data (such as the tariff and billing terms), contract terms (such as contract length and agreed testing length), the available SIM services (such as data, voice, SMS), and the technical provisioning data required to activate and operate SIMs. The products and services available in a package are itemised as separate <span>package items</span> on the customer invoice.</p>

<h2 id="Aboutpackageitems">About package items</h2>

<p>Your invoice details various package items and the price of consuming those services in a particular country.</p>

<p>SIM usage is charged monthly in arrears for both monthly and annual service charge intervals.</p>

<p>Package items may include:</p>

<ul> <li> Service charges. For more information, see <a href="/billing/billing-charges#Understa" target="_blank">About service charges</a>. </li> <li> SMS, data, and voice billing. For more information, see <a href="/billing/billing-sms-data-voice-billing" target="_blank">SMS, data, and voice billing</a>. <ul> <li> Bundled usage. For more information, see <a href="/billing/billing-pooling" target="_blank">Understanding pooling</a>. </li> </ul> </li> <li> Activation charges. For more information, see <a href="/billing/billing-charges#Activati" target="_blank">About activation charges</a>. </li> </ul>

<h3>Mobile Network Operator (MNO) billing</h3>

<p>If MNOs send out their invoices after the Eseye billing date, then these charges are deferred until the next monthly invoice.</p>
