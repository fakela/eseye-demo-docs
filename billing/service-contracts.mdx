<h2 id="Aboutservicecontracts">About service contracts</h2>

<p>A service contract defines the terms on which Eseye supplies a service to a customer. A service contract is created by linking a package to a customer portfolio.</p>

<p>A <span>package</span> contains the information required to supply, provision and operate SIMs for a customer. Each package is a specific bundle of products and services, tailored to meet particular customer needs. It defines commercial data (such as the tariff and billing terms), contract terms (such as contract length and agreed testing length), the available SIM services (such as data, voice, SMS), and the technical provisioning data required to activate and operate SIMs. The products and services available in a package are itemised as separate <span>package items</span> on the customer invoice.</p>

<h2 id="Prerequisitesforservicecontracts">Pre-requisites for service contracts</h2>

<p>A service contact is created when a customer is ready to operate a new or updated Eseye service.</p>

<p>Before a service contract is created, the following steps must be completed:</p>

<ul> <li> Creating a package that meets the commercial and technical requirements for the customer and Eseye </li> <li> Creating a customer account in Tigrillo </li> <li> Creating portfolios and sub-accounts in SIAM </li> </ul>

<Note>Service contracts can apply to named portfolios only, or to named portfolios and the sub-accounts within them.</Note>

<Note>A package can be assigned to portfolios for more than one customer. </Note>

<p>Packages should only be assigned to more than one customer in special cases. If the package is updated to meet one customer's needs (for example, to change how SIMs are provisioned or the country tariffs), this affects all customers using the package.</p>

<h2 id="Servicecontractlifecycle">Service contract lifecycle</h2>

<p>A service contract for a customer is created by linking a package to a named portfolio for the customer. </p>

<table> <col /> <col /> <thead> <tr> <th>Status</th> <th>Description</th> </tr> </thead> <tbody> <tr> <td>New</td> <td>A service contract has been created by linking a package to a portfolio.</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Unsigned</td> <td>The service contract has been sent to the customer for signing.</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Pending</td> <td> A customer has uploaded the signed service contract in SIAM. <br /> If the signed service contract is received from the customer directly and uploaded into Lynx, this status is skipped. </td> </tr> <tr> <td>Active</td> <td>The signed service contract has been received, checked and uploaded. </td> </tr> <tr> <td>Suspended</td> <td>Eseye wants to stop the customer activating any more SIMs on this package.</td> </tr> <tr> <td>Legacy</td> <td>The customer wants to stop activating any more SIMs on this package.</td> </tr> </tbody> </table>
